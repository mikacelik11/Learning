2. 🍃 Inside a Leaf

A leaf is like a tiny food factory. Inside, special structures capture sunlight, absorb water, and pull in CO₂ to make food. Chloroplasts are like little kitchens where plants cook their food using sunlight.

🟢 Chloroplast

This is where photosynthesis happens. Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll, the green pigment that captures sunlight. Think of them as tiny kitchens inside the leaf.

2

🕳️ Stomata

Tiny pores on the underside of the leaf. CO₂ enters here and oxygen exits. They open and close like little doors to control airflow.

3

💧 Veins

Veins carry water from the roots to the leaf and move glucose to the rest of the plant. Think of them as tiny pipes.

3. ☀️ What Do Plants Need?

Plants need three things to make food. Without any one of them, photosynthesis stops. Think of it like a recipe that needs all three ingredients.

🎙️
Listen: What Plants Need
Press play to hear this section read aloud.
☀️

Sunlight

Sunlight powers the whole process. Chlorophyll in leaves absorbs this energy to get photosynthesis going.

💧

Water (H₂O)

Roots absorb water from the soil and carry it up into the leaves. Water is broken apart during photosynthesis to help build glucose.

🌫️

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

CO₂ is a gas in the air that enters through the stomata. It's the same gas we breathe out. Plants use it as a building block for sugar.

4. 🌟 What Do Plants Make?

With sunlight, water, and CO₂, plants produce two things. One feeds the plant and one keeps the rest of us alive.

Glucose (Sugar)

Glucose is the plant's food. It gives the plant energy to grow and survive. Extra glucose gets stored in roots, fruits, or seeds.

🫧

Oxygen (O₂)

Oxygen is released into the air as a byproduct. Every breath you take comes from plants carrying out photosynthesis.

7. 🌍 Why Does It Matter?

Photosynthesis is the foundation of almost all life on Earth. Every time you breathe or eat, you can thank a plant.

🫁

We Breathe It

The oxygen you breathe was released by plants. Without photosynthesis, there would be no breathable air.

🍎

We Eat It

Plants turn glucose into fruits, vegetables, and grains. Animals eat plants too. Every meal you eat traces back to photosynthesis.

🌡️

Climate Balance

Plants absorb CO₂ from the air, which helps keep Earth's temperature stable and reduces the effects of climate change.

⚡

All Our Energy

Coal, oil, and gas come from ancient plant matter. Even fossil fuels started as sunlight captured by plants millions of years ago.

🧠 Think About It

What would happen to life on Earth if all plants disappeared? Think about food, oxygen, and climate. Discuss with a classmate!
