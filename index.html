🟢 Chloroplast

This is where photosynthesis actually happens! Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll — the green pigment that captures sunlight energy.

🕳️ Stomata

Tiny pores (holes) on the underside of the leaf. CO₂ enters here and oxygen exits. They open and close to control gas exchange.

💧 Veins

Leaf veins carry water up from the roots and move glucose away to the rest of the plant. Think of them like tiny pipes!

☀️ What Do Plants Need?

Plants need three key ingredients to make their own food. Click each card to learn more!

☀️

Sunlight

Energy from the sun powers the whole process. Chlorophyll in leaves absorbs sunlight to drive photosynthesis.

💧

Water (H₂O)

Absorbed by the roots and carried up through the stem. Water provides hydrogen atoms used to build glucose.

🌫️

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

A gas from the air. It enters the leaf through the stomata. Plants use CO₂ as a carbon source to build sugar.

🌟 What Do Plants Make?

Using sunlight, water, and CO₂, plants produce two important things.

🍬

Glucose (Sugar)

The plant's food! Glucose is used for energy to grow, reproduce, and carry out all life functions. It's also stored for later.

🫧

Oxygen (O₂)

Released into the air as a byproduct. This is the oxygen we breathe! Every breath you take is thanks to plants.

🌍 Why Does It Matter?

Photosynthesis isn't just about plants — it's the foundation of all life on Earth.

🫁

We Breathe It

Every oxygen molecule you breathe in was released by a plant or algae through photosynthesis.

🍎

We Eat It

The glucose plants make becomes fruit, vegetables, grains — and feeds animals too. All food traces back to photosynthesis.

🌡️

Climate Balance

Plants absorb CO₂ from the air, helping regulate Earth's temperature and reduce the effects of climate change.

⚡

All Our Energy

Coal, oil, and gas are ancient plant matter. Even our fossil fuels started as sunlight captured by photosynthesis millions of years ago.
